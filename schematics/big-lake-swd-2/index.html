Critical correction: the previous revision was geometrically impossible. It placed the future packer at ~3,565 ft while the active disposal zone already begins at 3,458 ft, and it let the proposed production interval overlap the disposal interval by 106 ft. This revision removes that conflict. The future concept now assumes production interval 2 is shortened to 3,380-3,448 ft and the new dual-bore packer is landed in the narrow 3,450-3,458 ft window, pending log confirmation.
FUTURE STATE - A corrected parallel-string dual completion. One short string delivers power fluid down to the jet pump. One long string carries disposal fluid down through the dual-bore packer and discharges below the packer, above the active disposal interval. Produced oil and spent power fluid return up the annulus above the packer to the production outlet.

Packer depth

Resolved
The current packer is treated as a Versatile On/Off unit from the 2023 workover era. Legacy D&L references should not be carried into the current-state narrative.
3

Active disposal perforations

Resolved
Correct active disposal intervals are five intervals from 3,458-3,834 ft totaling 318 ft. The simplified two-interval representation is no longer used as the current basis.
4

PBTD / fill

Resolved
Current PBTD is 4,002 ft. The old 3,820 ft sediment reference was legacy original-state information, not current well condition.
5

Current tubing spec and volumetrics

Resolved
Current tubing is 4-1/2 in. EUE polycore, 12.75 lb/ft, 3.4 in. minimum ID. The old 0.00579 bbl/ft number was removed as a current-state value and replaced with 0.01123 bbl/ft.
6

Future-state geometry

Corrected
The prior future-state drawing had three internal contradictions: (1) packer below the top of disposal, (2) production interval overlapping disposal interval, and (3) injection discharge inconsistent with the new packer location. Rev. 7 corrects that by:
  • shortening production interval 2 to 3,380-3,448 ft
  • moving the target dual-bore packer window to ~3,450-3,458 ft
  • requiring injection discharge to remain below the packer
Final placement still needs field confirmation.
