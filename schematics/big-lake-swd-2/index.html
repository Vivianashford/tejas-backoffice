FUTURE STATE — After the dual-completion workover, the well produces oil from the upper zone (3,200–3,564 ft, newly perforated in two intervals) via a jet pump, while simultaneously continuing permitted waste injection into the lower zone (3,458–3,834 ft existing perfs). This is a parallel-string dual completion through a dual-bore packer: the power-fluid string lands the jet pump just above the packer; the injection string passes through the packer to discharge above the disposal perfs; and the tubing-casing annulus above the packer returns commingled oil + spent power fluid to the production outlet at the dual wellhead.
!
Critical Geometry Finding — Packer Must Be Reset
The existing packer at 3,120 ft cannot serve the dual completion as drawn. Production perfs proposed at 3,200–3,564 ft must be ABOVE the packer (so they communicate with the annulus return to the production outlet), and disposal perfs at 3,458–3,834 ft must be BELOW it. The packer must therefore sit between the two zones at roughly 3,565 ft. The current 2023 packer must be pulled and a dual-bore packer set at the new depth. Budget impact: $30K–$80K (already in the Gate 4 workover budget — see Well Plan §5.2 Risk 4).
